Bad Website: Arngren

The first time the user enters the website, there are a couple of problems with consistency. Firstly, image sizes are different across the page and do not sit on a proper template. Instead, it is scattered all around the page. Secondly, every advertisement has a different colour, font style, and size, making it eye-soring to see what you are looking for. On a different note, some paddings of the pictures are blue while others are orange, disrupting the consistency. Furthermore, there are three search bars on the website in very different areas; you cannot find them without a magnifying glass. The company's contact information is twice in various font sizes and colours. Overall, this website is not consistent. There are also some problems with the ease of navigation for the users. Pictures with different sizes, unorganized orders and layouts make it hard for viewers to locate what they want. Some links to specific items are too small to click, and some are located between two frames of the items, so it confuses the viewers of which item the link belongs to. The most inconvenient part is the horizontal scrolling. It is hard to navigate on the web page.

Good Website: Hack The North

Hack the North's website maintains a consistent paper/hand-drawn theme, creating a cohesive and visually engaging experience. Key design elements include a fixed navbar, 2D animations, and uniform typography, with common h1, h2, and p styles ensuring readability. Each section follows a structured margin system, and h1 tags are centered with a distinct “paper” component. The layout is built with Flexbox, ensuring responsive and adaptive design. Navigation is straightforward, with the navbar linking directly to key sections. However, one usability critique was the UI for the “Last Year's Winners” section, which could be more intuitive. Another minor issue is clickability of sponsor images, which may benefit from clearer interaction cues. The site promotes universal usability with a well-structured header and footer, ensuring accessibility and ease of use across devices.
